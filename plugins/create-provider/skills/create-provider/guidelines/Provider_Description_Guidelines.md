# Provider Description Guidelines

## Overview
This document provides guidelines for writing Provider descriptions that focus on the **organization/company** rather than the products/platforms they offer. Descriptions should explain what kind of company the Provider is, where they're located, and what they develop or produce.

---

## Core Principle

**Provider descriptions are about the ORGANIZATION, not the PRODUCT.**

- ✅ **Correct**: "Slack Technologies is a business software company based in San Francisco that develops workplace communication tools..."
- ❌ **Wrong**: "Slack is a messaging platform that allows teams to communicate through channels..."

The description should answer: **"What kind of company is this?"** not **"What does their product do?"**

---

## Description Structure

### Recommended Format (30-90 words)

```
[Company Name] is a [company type] [industry descriptor] based in [location] that [what they develop/produce/do]. The company [brief mention of main product/service]. [Optional: Additional context about operations, business model, or notable characteristics].
```

### Template Breakdown

**1. Company Identity (Required)**
- Legal/brand name
- Company type: technology company, software vendor, open-source foundation, nonprofit organization, etc.
- Industry/sector: technology, software, enterprise software, etc.
- Note: Avoid redundant qualifiers like "commercial" unless distinguishing from nonprofit

**2. Location (If Known)**
- Headquarters city and state/country
- Example: "based in San Francisco, California"

**3. What They Develop/Produce (Required)**
- Focus on WHAT THE COMPANY DOES, not what the product does
- Use verbs like: develops, produces, creates, maintains, provides, operates
- Example: "develops workplace communication software" NOT "provides messaging features"

**4. Product/Service Mention (Optional, Brief)**
- ONE sentence maximum mentioning main product/platform
- Keep it general, not feature-focused
- Example: "The company offers a web-based platform for team collaboration"

**5. Additional Context (Optional)**
- Business model (SaaS, open-source, consulting)
- Notable characteristics (enterprise-focused, startup, established vendor)
- Scale indicators if relevant (serves Fortune 500, global operations)

## Examples from Different Provider Types

### Example 1: Enterprise Technology Company ✅

**Provider**: Slack Technologies

**Good Description (Organization-Focused)**:
"Slack Technologies is a software company based in San Francisco, California that develops workplace communication tools. The company provides a cloud-based collaboration platform used by teams and organizations for messaging, file sharing, and workflow integration. Slack operates as a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) provider serving enterprise and small business customers globally."

**Word count**: 53 words
**Focus**: Company type (software company), location (SF), what they develop (workplace communication), brief product mention (collaboration platform), business model (SaaS)

---

**Bad Description (Product-Focused)** ❌:
"Slack is a messaging platform that allows teams to communicate through channels, direct messages, and threads. The platform integrates with over 2,000 apps and services, offers searchable message history, file sharing, video calls, and workflow automation through Slack bots. Users can create custom workflows and automate routine tasks."

**Why it's wrong**:
- Describes product features, not the company
- No company type or location
- Reads like a product spec sheet
- Doesn't explain what kind of organization Slack is

---

### Example 2: Open-Source Foundation ✅

**Provider**: Apache Software Foundation

**Good Description (Organization-Focused)**:
"The Apache Software Foundation is a nonprofit organization based in Delaware, United States that develops and maintains open-source software projects. The foundation operates through a community-driven model with over 350 projects covering web servers, big data, cloud computing, and enterprise software. Apache is governed by a decentralized structure where project communities operate independently under the foundation's umbrella."

**Word count**: 60 words
**Focus**: Organization type (nonprofit), location (Delaware), what they do (develop/maintain OSS), scope (350 projects), governance model

---

### Example 3: Individual Developer ✅

**Provider**: Petri Lehtinen

**Good Description (Organization-Focused)**:
"Petri Lehtinen is an individual software developer based in Finland who creates and maintains open-source libraries for C and Python programming. Lehtinen is known for developing Jansson, a widely-used JSON library for C, and contributes to various open-source projects in the data serialization and parsing domains."

**Word count**: 50 words
**Focus**: Individual developer, location (Finland), what they create (OSS libraries), notable work (Jansson)

---

### Example 4: SaaS Startup ✅

**Provider**: TeamSmart.ai

**Good Description (Organization-Focused)**:
"TeamSmart.ai is a technology company based in San Francisco, California that develops AI software products. The company offers a web-based platform that provides access to AI models and specialized assistants for business applications, including tools for content creation, code generation, and workflow automation."

**Word count**: 48 words
**Focus**: Company type (technology company), location (SF), what they develop (AI software), brief product mention (web platform with AI models)

## What to Focus On

### ✅ DO Include:

1. **Company Type/Classification**
   - Examples: "commercial software company", "open-source foundation", "technology startup", "individual developer", "consulting firm"

2. **Location/Geography**
   - Headquarters city and country
   - "based in [City, State/Country]"
   - Include if available; omit if not found

3. **What the Company Develops/Produces**
   - Software, hardware, services, platforms
   - Industry focus: enterprise software, developer tools, consumer apps
   - Technology domains: AI, cloud computing, cybersecurity, etc.

4. **Business Model (if relevant)**
   - SaaS, open-source, consulting, licensing
   - Enterprise vs. consumer focus
   - Nonprofit vs. commercial

5. **Scale/Scope Indicators (if notable)**
   - "serves Fortune 500 companies"
   - "operates globally"
   - "maintains 350+ projects"

### ❌ DO NOT Include:

1. **Detailed Product Features**
   - Feature lists ("offers channels, threads, reactions, file sharing...")
   - UI/UX descriptions ("clean interface with dark mode...")
   - Technical specifications ("supports OAuth 2.0, REST API...")

2. **Marketing Language**
   - Buzzwords: "seamless", "cutting-edge", "revolutionary", "innovative"
   - Superlatives: "best", "leading", "most advanced"
   - Benefits claims: "boost productivity", "transform workflows"

3. **Pricing/Plans**
   - "Free tier available"
   - "Starting at $9/month"
   - "Enterprise pricing"

4. **Customer Testimonials**
   - "Trusted by 10,000+ companies"
   - "Rated 4.5 stars"

5. **Competitive Positioning**
   - "Better than [Competitor]"
   - "Alternative to [Product]"

6. **Product Roadmap**
   - "Coming soon features"
   - "Plans to expand"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Feature List Instead of Company Overview ❌

**Wrong**:
"Jira provides issue tracking, project management, sprint planning, kanban boards, custom workflows, advanced reporting, time tracking, roadmaps, and integrates with 3,000+ tools."

**Correct**:
"Atlassian is an Australian enterprise software company that develops collaboration and project management tools. The company provides software products including Jira for issue tracking, Confluence for documentation, and Bitbucket for code management, primarily serving software development teams and IT organizations."

---

### Mistake 2: Starting with Product Name Instead of Company ❌

**Wrong**:
"WordPress is a content management system used by millions of websites for blogging and web publishing. It offers themes, plugins, and customizable templates."

**Correct**:
"WordPress is an open-source content management system developed by a global community of contributors and maintained by Automattic, a web development company. The software powers websites and blogs through a plugin-based architecture and theming system."

---

### Mistake 3: Too Product-Specific, No Company Context ❌

**Wrong**:
"Provides AI-powered chatbots, natural language processing, sentiment analysis, conversation analytics, and multi-channel support for customer service teams."

**Correct**:
"Intercom is a customer communication platform company based in San Francisco that develops software for business-to-customer messaging. The company provides tools for sales, marketing, and support teams to communicate with customers through web, mobile, and email channels."

---

### Mistake 4: Marketing Speak Instead of Facts ❌

**Wrong**:
"Notion revolutionizes productivity with its seamless all-in-one workspace that transforms how teams collaborate. The cutting-edge platform empowers organizations to boost efficiency and streamline workflows."

**Correct**:
"Notion Labs is a productivity software company based in San Francisco that develops workspace and collaboration tools. The company offers a web-based platform combining note-taking, task management, databases, and wikis for individuals and teams."

## Word Count Guidelines

**Target**: 30-90 words

### Why 30 words minimum?
- Ensures sufficient context about the organization
- Allows for company type + location + what they do + brief product mention
- Avoids overly terse descriptions that lack information

### Why 90 words maximum?
- Keeps descriptions concise and scannable
- Prevents feature-list bloat
- Focuses on essentials rather than comprehensive detail

### Typical Distribution:
- **30-50 words**: Simple providers (individual developers, single-product companies)
- **50-70 words**: Standard enterprises (most common)
- **70-90 words**: Complex organizations (foundations with many projects, conglomerates)

## Provider Category Considerations

Different provider categories need slightly different emphasis:

### Enterprise Companies
**Focus**: Company structure, location, industry, business model

**Example**:
"Salesforce is an enterprise cloud computing company based in San Francisco, California that develops customer relationship management (CRM) software. The company operates a Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) platform for sales, service, marketing, and analytics, serving businesses across industries globally."

---

### Community-Based / Open Source
**Focus**: Organization type, governance, project scope, community structure

**Example**:
"The Linux Foundation is a nonprofit technology consortium based in San Francisco that supports open-source software development. The organization hosts and maintains critical infrastructure projects including the Linux kernel, Kubernetes, and Node.js, operating through a community-driven governance model with corporate and individual members."

---

### Individual Developers
**Focus**: Developer identity, location (if public), specialty/expertise, notable projects

**Example**:
"Evan You is an independent software developer known for creating Vue.js, a progressive JavaScript framework for building user interfaces. You maintains Vue.js and its ecosystem as open-source projects supported by community contributions and sponsorship."

---

## Verification Checklist

Before finalizing a Provider description, verify:

- [ ] **Organization-focused**: Describes the company/entity, not the product
- [ ] **Company type stated**: Commercial, nonprofit, individual, foundation, etc.
- [ ] **Location included** (if available): City, state/province, country
- [ ] **What they develop/produce**: Clear statement of what the organization does
- [ ] **Product mention brief** (if included): One sentence max, not feature-focused
- [ ] **Word count 30-90**: Within target range
- [ ] **No marketing language**: Removed buzzwords (seamless, revolutionary, etc.)
- [ ] **Factual tone**: Objective, descriptive, not promotional
- [ ] **No feature lists**: Avoids detailed product specifications
- [ ] **Grammatically correct**: Proper sentences, no typos

## Sources for Description Content

### Priority Order:

1. **Official "About Us" page**
   - Company history, mission, what they do
   - Usually contains organization-level information

2. **LinkedIn "About" section**
   - Company profile often has organization focus
   - Industry, company size, headquarters

3. **Crunchbase profile**
   - Company overview, funding, headquarters
   - Business model and market focus

4. **Wikipedia (if available)**
   - Usually organization-focused with history
   - Verify citations, don't copy directly

5. **Press releases / Company blog**
   - "About [Company]" sections in announcements
   - Founding stories, company milestones

### What NOT to Use as Primary Source:

- ❌ Product landing pages (too product-focused)
- ❌ Feature comparison charts
- ❌ Customer testimonials or case studies
- ❌ Pricing pages
- ❌ Product documentation

## Rewriting Product-Focused Descriptions

If you find yourself with a product-focused description, rewrite using this process:

### Step 1: Identify the Organization
- What kind of company is this?
- Where are they located?
- What do they develop/produce?

### Step 2: Remove Product Features
- Delete feature lists
- Delete "how it works" explanations
- Delete UI/UX descriptions

### Step 3: Add Organization Context
- Company type (commercial, nonprofit, individual)
- Location (headquarters)
- What the organization does (develops X for Y)

### Step 4: Optionally Mention Product (Briefly)
- One sentence about main product/platform
- Keep it general, not feature-specific

### Example Transformation:

**Before (Product-Focused)** ❌:
"Figma is a collaborative design tool that allows teams to create, prototype, and share designs in real-time. The platform offers vector editing, prototyping, design systems, comments and feedback, version history, and integrates with development tools."

**After (Organization-Focused)** ✅:
"Figma is a design software company based in San Francisco, California that develops cloud-based design and prototyping tools. The company provides a web-based platform for interface design and collaboration, primarily serving product design teams, UI/UX designers, and software developers."

## Special Cases

### Case 1: Product Name = Company Name

When the product and company share the same name:

**Pattern**:
"[Name] is a [company type] based in [location] that develops [product category]. The company provides [brief product description]."

**Example**:
"Trello is a project management software company owned by Atlassian that develops task organization tools. The company provides a web-based application using a kanban-style interface for managing projects and workflows."

---

### Case 2: Acquired Companies

When a provider was acquired:

**Pattern**:
"[Name] is a [company type], now owned by [Parent Company], that develops [what they do]. The company [brief product mention]."

**Example**:
"GitHub is a software development platform company, now owned by Microsoft, that provides hosting services for version control using Git. The company operates a web-based platform for code collaboration, issue tracking, and project management."

---

### Case 3: Conglomerates with Multiple Products

When a company has many products:

**Pattern**:
"[Name] is a [company type] based in [location] that develops [product category]. The company provides [product line/portfolio description] for [target market]."

**Example**:
"Adobe is a multinational software company based in San Jose, California that develops creative and marketing software products. The company provides applications for graphic design, video editing, web development, and digital marketing, serving creative professionals and enterprises globally."

---

### Case 4: No Company Information Available

If you cannot find organization-level information:

**Fallback Pattern**:
"[Name] is a [inferred type] that provides [product/service]. [Brief additional context if available]."

**Example**:
"Appsmith is a low-code development platform for building internal tools and applications. The platform allows developers to create custom business applications using a drag-and-drop interface with database connections."

**Note**: This is acceptable when no company information exists, but always attempt organization research first.

## Quick Reference

### Organization-Focused Language:

✅ "is a software company that develops..."
✅ "is a technology company based in San Francisco that creates..."
✅ "operates as a SaaS provider serving..."
✅ "maintains open-source projects for..."
✅ "is a nonprofit organization that supports..."

### Product-Focused Language (Avoid):

❌ "offers channels, threads, and reactions..."
❌ "allows users to create and share..."
❌ "provides features such as..."
❌ "includes advanced analytics, reporting..."
❌ "enables teams to collaborate through..."

## Final Reminder

**Ask yourself**: "Am I describing the COMPANY or the PRODUCT?"

If your description could be copied directly to the product landing page → **It's product-focused, rewrite it.**

If your description explains what kind of organization this is and what they do → **It's organization-focused, good to go.**
